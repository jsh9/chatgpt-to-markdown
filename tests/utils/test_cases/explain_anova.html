1. The Big Idea

ANOVA tests whether the means of several groups are equal.

Suppose we have kkk groups, each with sample size nin_ini​, and overall sample size N=∑i=1kniN = \sum_{i=1}^k n_iN=∑i=1k​ni​.

  • Null hypothesis:

H0:μ1=μ2=⋯=μkH_0: \mu_1 = \mu_2 = \cdots = \mu_kH0​:μ1​=μ2​=⋯=μk​

  • Alternative hypothesis:

Ha:At least one mean differsH_a: \text{At least one mean differs}Ha​:At least one mean differs

2. Partitioning Variability

The total variation in the data is split into:

Total Variation=Variation Between Groups+Variation Within Groups\text{Total Variation} = \text{Variation Between Groups} + \text{Variation Within Groups}Total Variation=Variation Between Groups+Variation Within Groups

This is the ANOVA identity:

SST=SSB+SSWSS_T = SS_B + SS_WSST​=SSB​+SSW​

  • SSTSS_TSST​ = Total Sum of Squares
